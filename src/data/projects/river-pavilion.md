A small public pavilion at the water's edge, drawing on the traditional Suzhou garden practice of borrowed scenery. The structure intervenes with minimal material, framing water, distant hills and sky into composed views.

The mono-pitched roof tilts toward the water, pressing the eave line low to unconsciously direct the visitor's gaze. What is kept out is as deliberate as what is let in.
