A private retreat nestled in the mountains, drawing upon traditional timber joinery to create a dialogue between shelter and landscape. The structure breathes with its site, opening toward the valley at dawn and closing inward by dusk.

The roof follows the mountain ridge line in a gentle slope, while the interior retains the natural character of raw timber and rammed earth — materials that carry the visible marks of time. Each joint is resolved by hand, a form of construction that slows the process and deepens the maker's relationship to place.
